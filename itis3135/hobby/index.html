How

Depending on what music I am making, I might use a computer or a guitar. Usually I use a computer, but sometimes I have written some music or riffs on either guitar or piano. I have a mini keyboard I can plug into my computer, and a full 88 key piano back at home that I use and practice. I can also plug my guitar into my computer to record and practice.

The program I use when making music is FL Studio. Ive been using it for years, however only more recently have I been using it more often, since I found out how to use soundfonts. Soundfonts are a collection of instruments in the sf2 file format that is used to make sound in FL Studio. I have a collection of soundfonts that I use when I make music, mainly from old school videogames. My favorite soundfonts are the Touhou soundfonts, the Final Fantasy 7 soundfonts, and the PC-98 soundfont. These contain a wide variety of both live sounding instruments, and old sounding instruments.

I play with a Seafoam Green Squier Stratocaster. Ive been wanting to upgrade my guitar, but this one gets the job done. It has a nice tone that I like, and the colors are awesome. I play on bridge pickups, and tend to pick as close as I can to the bridge to give the guitar a twangy and sphagetti tone that is heard in surf guitar and in jangle guitar. When it comes to guitar effects I have two sounds I like. One is a jangley sound, and the other is distorted. For my jangley sound I use chorus with both rate and width at around 1oClock, and I use spring reverb with knob at 12oClock. My distorted sound has bass and mids at 2oClock, and treble at 1oClock. This is because I do not like when distorted guitar has too much treble and too little bass. It's one of my issues with a lot of metal music as that is the guitar tone in most metal music. Punk has amazing tones as its usually very punchy and has a good amount of bass and mids. I usually put the distortion knob low (8oClock)since I don't like it being too loud nor too distorted.
